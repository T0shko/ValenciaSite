Granada

Discover the Soul of Andalusia

Nestled at the foot of the Sierra Nevada mountains, Granada is one of Spain’s most iconic and culturally rich cities. With its blend of Moorish heritage, Renaissance architecture, and vibrant modern life, Granada offers visitors an unforgettable journey through time, culture, and landscape.

Where Is Granada?

Granada is located in the autonomous community of Andalusia, in southern Spain. It lies about an hour from the Mediterranean coast and just 40 minutes from the Sierra Nevada ski resort, making it a unique crossroads between sea and snow.

The Alhambra – Jewel of Islamic Architecture

Granada is world-famous for the Alhambra, a majestic palace and fortress complex built during the Nasrid dynasty in the 13th and 14th centuries. This UNESCO World Heritage Site is one of the most visited monuments in Europe, attracting millions with its intricate stucco work, serene courtyards, and breathtaking views over the city.

A City of Cultures

Granada’s history is a fascinating blend of Islamic, Jewish, and Christian influences. The city was the last stronghold of the Moors in Spain, falling to the Catholic Monarchs Ferdinand and Isabella in 1492. This multicultural legacy is reflected in its architecture, cuisine, and traditions.
